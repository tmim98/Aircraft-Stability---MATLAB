Second-pass digitization of Nelson 2nd ed. Fig. 2.29

Files:
- wingBody_kn_from_fig229_v2.m
- figure_2_29_digitized_samples_v2.csv

Method:
1) Re-digitize the three line-family blocks from tighter crops supplied later.
2) Upper-left block:
   - Estimate each l_f^2/S_fs family line at two reference x-columns.
   - Interpolate linearly between the two columns.
3) Upper-right block:
   - Re-estimate the five sqrt(h1/h2) rays near their common pivot.
4) Lower-right block:
   - Re-estimate the five h/w_f rays and the k_n vertical scale.
5) Chain the three blocks as a nomogram.

Why this version should be better:
- The common-pivot regions in the upper-right and lower-right blocks were re-read
  from tighter crops, because those regions are most sensitive to small pixel errors.
- The upper-left line family was re-estimated from crop-specific coordinates rather
  than reusing the full-figure coordinates.

Important:
- This is still a digitization from a screenshot of a PDF, so treat it as an
  engineering approximation rather than a canonical source.
- The best way to build confidence is to compare this function against 10-20 fresh
  hand-read points, especially near the pivot region.
